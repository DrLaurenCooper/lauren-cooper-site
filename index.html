My story

I was a first-generation student from a working-class background who was always told I was too sensitive. I self-funded a PhD in English Literature and discovered that teaching was my passion, but traditional academia didn't provide the nervous system safety I need. Nor did it give me enough space to do the work I loved most: not simply teaching literature but empowering students to be creative, expressive, and confident.

Throughout my own struggles, literature has been my constant, getting me through the darkest experiences. As I've spent decades trying to understand and heal myself, journaling and self-care have become just as integral to my existence, and I deeply believe that I am on this planet to share my insights and tools with others.

Through workshops, courses and 1:1 work, I get to create sacred containers where people explore literature and their inner worlds—spaces for creative expression, deep reflection, and being truly held.
